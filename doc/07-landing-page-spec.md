# Landing Page Specification — LockUp

Version: 1.0  
Status: Conversion System Source of Truth  
Goal: Convert visitors into committed users

---

## 1. Purpose

The landing page is not informational.

It is a **behavioral persuasion system** designed to:

- Communicate discipline as the core value
- Create emotional tension around distraction
- Position LockUp as a necessity, not a tool
- Drive sign-ups and first session creation

---

## 2. Core Principle

> “The landing page must make inaction feel uncomfortable.”

Users should feel:

- They are wasting time
- They lack discipline structure
- LockUp is the solution to that gap

---

## 3. Audience

Primary:

- University students (16–30)
- Distracted, ambitious individuals

Secondary:

- Developers
- Freelancers
- Remote workers

---

## 4. Conversion Goal

Primary CTA:

> Start Your First Focus Session

Secondary CTA:

> Join LockUp

Everything else supports this.

---

## 5. Page Structure

---

### 5.1 Hero Section (Critical)

Purpose:
Immediate emotional positioning

---

#### Hero Headline

> Discipline Beats Motivation.

---

#### Subheadline

Lock distracting apps, protect your focus, and build discipline one session at a time.

---

#### Visual Concept

- Dark calm background
- Subtle glowing focus orb or timer
- Minimal UI preview of session lock state

---

#### Primary CTA

- “Start First Session”

Secondary CTA:

- “See How It Works”

---

### 5.2 Problem Section

Purpose:
Make user self-aware

---

Content:

Users are constantly distracted.

- Social media interrupts focus
- Motivation fails daily
- Productivity apps don’t enforce behavior

---

Emotional framing:

> “You don’t need more tools. You need discipline enforcement.”

---

### 5.3 Solution Section

Purpose:
Introduce LockUp system

---

LockUp solves this by:

- Blocking distractions during focus
- Enforcing commitment through lock levels
- Rewarding consistency with XP
- Tracking discipline over time

---

### 5.4 How It Works

Step-based flow:

1. Create a focus session
2. Select apps to block
3. Choose lock level
4. Start session
5. Stay focused until completion

---

### 5.5 Lock Levels Explanation

---

#### Level 1 — Flexible

- Exit anytime
- No penalty

---

#### Level 2 — Commitment

- Exit allowed
- XP penalty applied
- Streak risk introduced

---

#### Level 3 — Strict

- Cannot exit
- Must complete session

---

### 5.6 Benefits Section

Focus outcomes:

- Better discipline
- Higher consistency
- Reduced distraction
- Identity shift (“I am focused”)

---

### 5.7 Social Proof Section (Optional MVP Light)

- “Users complete 2x more focus sessions”
- “Streak consistency improves within 7 days”

(If no real data exists yet, use placeholder messaging carefully)

---

### 5.8 CTA Section (Final Conversion Push)

Message:

> Your discipline starts with one session.

Button:

Start Focus Session

---

## 6. Visual Direction

Landing page must feel:

- Calm but intense
- Minimal but premium
- Focused, not busy

---

### Design Rules

- Dark or muted backgrounds
- One accent color per section
- Large spacing between sections
- Strong typography hierarchy
- No clutter or excessive visuals

---

## 7. Motion Guidelines

Motion must:

- Guide attention downward
- Reinforce flow progression
- Animate CTA subtly
- Provide feedback on scroll transitions

Avoid:

- Distracting animations
- Overuse of motion effects

---

## 8. UX Psychology Principles

---

### 8.1 Loss Awareness

User should feel:

> “I am losing focus time right now.”

---

### 8.2 Identity Trigger

Landing page should push:

> “Disciplined people use systems like this.”

---

### 8.3 Simplicity Bias

No complexity in messaging.

One idea per section.

---

## 9. Performance Requirements

- Fast load (<2.5s target)
- Minimal assets
- Optimized images
- Mobile-first rendering

---

## 10. Mobile Priority Rules

- CTA always visible or easily reachable
- Sections stack vertically
- Large tap targets
- Minimal text density

---

## 11. Anti-Patterns

DO NOT:

- Over-explain features
- Add too many CTAs
- Use flashy marketing gimmicks
- Clutter the hero section
- Mix too many ideas per section

---

## 12. Final Principle

The landing page is not marketing.

It is behavioral framing.

> It does not sell an app.
> It sells a new identity: discipline.
