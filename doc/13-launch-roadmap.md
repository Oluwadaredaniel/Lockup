# Launch Roadmap — LockUp

Version: 1.0  
Status: Execution Plan  
Scope: MVP → Launch → Scale Strategy

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how LockUp is built, released, and scaled.

It ensures:

- Controlled complexity growth
- Early validation of discipline system
- Stable MVP delivery
- Scalable long-term roadmap

---

## 2. Core Principle

> “Ship discipline first, features second.”

LockUp is not validated by features.

It is validated by:

- Whether users complete focus sessions
- Whether streaks improve behavior
- Whether consistency increases

---

## 3. Phased Development Strategy

---

## PHASE 1 — FOUNDATION (MVP BUILD)

---

### Goal

Build the core discipline loop.

---

### Features

- Authentication (Email, Google, Apple)
- Dashboard (basic discipline overview)
- Focus Session creation
- Active Focus Session screen
- Session completion system
- XP system (basic)
- Streak system (basic)
- Firestore integration

---

### Success Criteria

- Users can start and complete sessions
- XP is awarded correctly
- Streak increments daily
- No critical crashes

---

### Non-Goals

- No social features
- No advanced analytics
- No leaderboards
- No AI coach

---

## PHASE 2 — DISCIPLINE ENHANCEMENT

---

### Goal

Strengthen behavioral enforcement.

---

### Features

- Lock Levels (1–3 fully implemented)
- App blocking system improvements
- Discipline Score system
- Notification system
- Recovery flow (fail handling)

---

### Focus

This phase makes LockUp:

> harder to bypass, easier to trust

---

## PHASE 3 — RETENTION SYSTEMS

---

### Goal

Increase long-term usage.

---

### Features

- Achievements system
- Progress dashboard
- XP history visualization
- Streak analytics
- Weekly discipline reports

---

### Outcome

Users begin forming identity shift:

> “I am someone who completes sessions.”

---

## PHASE 4 — EXPERIENCE POLISH

---

### Goal

Make product feel premium and world-class.

---

### Features

- Motion system refinements
- UI polish across all screens
- Micro-interactions
- Improved onboarding experience
- Performance optimization

---

### Standard

Must feel like:

- Linear-level polish
- Apple-level consistency
- Duolingo-level feedback loops

---

## PHASE 5 — SCALE FEATURES

---

### Goal

Expand ecosystem.

---

### Features (optional expansion)

- Social accountability system
- Friends/study groups
- Leaderboards
- AI discipline coach
- Advanced analytics dashboard
- Web dashboard

---

## 4. MVP Definition (CRITICAL)

---

LockUp MVP =

> “User can consistently start and complete focus sessions with tracked discipline outcomes.”

If this fails → nothing else matters.

---

## 5. Release Strategy

---

### Internal Testing

- Test session flow stability
- Validate XP and streak logic
- Ensure session recovery works

---

### Closed Beta

- University students
- Focus-heavy users
- Feedback collection on discipline behavior

---

### Public Beta

- Wider audience
- Behavior tracking optimization
- Retention measurement

---

### Public Launch

- App Store release
- Marketing push
- Content-based acquisition

---

## 6. Growth Strategy

---

LockUp growth is NOT feature-driven.

It is behavior-driven:

- “People want discipline”
- “People struggle with focus”
- “LockUp enforces structure”

---

## 7. Success Metrics

---

### Behavioral Metrics

- Daily session completion rate
- 7-day streak retention
- Session abandonment reduction
- Average focus duration increase

---

### Product Metrics

- DAU / MAU
- Session frequency per user
- Retention (D1, D7, D30)

---

## 8. Risk Strategy

---

### Risk 1 — Users bypass system

Mitigation:

- Server-side enforcement
- Strict session validation

---

### Risk 2 — Low engagement

Mitigation:

- Strong onboarding loop
- Immediate first session success

---

### Risk 3 — Over-complexity

Mitigation:

- Keep MVP minimal
- Delay advanced features

---

## 9. Development Principle

---

> “If it does not improve discipline behavior, it is not part of MVP.”

---

## 10. Final Principle

LockUp is not built to be completed.

It is built to evolve behavior over time.

> Build the loop first. Everything else comes later.
