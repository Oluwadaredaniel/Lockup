# Brand Guidelines — LockUp

Version: 1.0  
Status: Identity Source of Truth  
Scope: Messaging, tone, personality, communication rules

---

## 1. Brand Essence

LockUp is a discipline system.

Not a productivity tool.  
Not a habit tracker.  
Not a planner.

It is a **behavior enforcement identity system**.

---

## 2. Core Brand Idea

> “Discipline is identity, not motivation.”

LockUp exists to transform users into people who:

- Keep commitments
- Resist distraction
- Build consistency
- Trust their own discipline

---

## 3. Brand Personality

LockUp is:

- Calm
- Serious (not harsh)
- Structured
- Encouraging through truth
- Focus-oriented
- Premium in tone

LockUp is NOT:

- Motivational fluff
- Overly friendly or childish
- Aggressive or shaming
- Corporate and robotic

---

## 4. Brand Voice

---

### 4.1 Tone Principles

Speak like:

- A disciplined mentor
- A calm accountability system
- A structured guide

Never speak like:

- A hype coach
- A productivity influencer
- A casual social app

---

### 4.2 Communication Style

- Short sentences
- Clear meaning
- No unnecessary words
- Direct feedback
- Emotionally controlled

---

## 5. Messaging Philosophy

---

### 5.1 Core Message

> “You do not need more motivation. You need discipline.”

---

### 5.2 Supporting Messages

- “Focus is built through consistency.”
- “Every session counts.”
- “Discipline is earned daily.”
- “Your habits define your identity.”

---

## 6. Emotional Positioning

LockUp should make users feel:

- Calm pressure (not anxiety)
- Responsibility
- Awareness of time usage
- Motivation through structure
- Identity shift over time

---

## 7. Writing Rules

---

### 7.1 Do’s

- Use simple language
- Be direct and honest
- Focus on behavior, not features
- Reinforce discipline identity
- Use active voice

---

### 7.2 Don’ts

- No motivational clichés
- No “you can do it” style messaging
- No exaggerated hype language
- No emotional manipulation
- No childish phrasing

---

## 8. Microcopy Guidelines

---

### 8.1 Buttons

Use action-based language:

- “Start Session”
- “Begin Focus”
- “Continue Discipline”

Avoid:

- “Let’s go!”
- “Ready?”
- “Click here”

---

### 8.2 Errors

Must be:

- Neutral
- Informative
- Non-blaming

Example:

> “Session interrupted. Your progress has been saved.”

Not:

> “You failed your session.”

---

### 8.3 Success States

Must reinforce identity:

> “Session completed. Discipline strengthened.”

Not:

> “Good job!”

---

## 9. Notification Tone

Notifications must:

- Be minimal
- Be purposeful
- Reinforce discipline behavior

Examples:

- “Your focus session starts in 5 minutes.”
- “You are one day away from breaking your streak.”
- “Time to complete your commitment.”

---

## 10. Naming Conventions

---

### Features

Must reflect discipline behavior:

- Focus Session (not Timer)
- Discipline Score (not Points)
- Lock Level (not Difficulty Mode)
- Streak (not Login Days)

---

## 11. Brand Differentiation

LockUp is NOT:

- Duolingo (fun-first gamification)
- Forest (visual meditation focus)
- Notion (productivity organization)

LockUp is:

> A behavioral discipline enforcement system disguised as a simple app

---

## 12. Identity Goal

Users should eventually think:

> “I don’t skip sessions anymore. That’s just who I am.”

---

## 13. Final Principle

Brand is not marketing.

Brand is behavior shaping through language.

In LockUp:

> Every word must reinforce discipline identity.
